Penang Hill

Introduction

Penang Hill is a hill resort on Penang Island, Malaysia, with multiple peaks including Flagstaff Hill and Strawberry Hill. It stands out with its forested, hilly terrain, offering historical significance from the British colonial period. A major tourist attraction, the summit of Flagstaff Hill is accessible through the unique Penang Hill Railway. The site is part of the Penang Hill Biosphere Reserve, designated by UNESCO as Malaysia's third Biosphere Reserve on the World Network of Biosphere Reserves.

Rise early to catch the first funicular trip up the hill and be rewarded by breath taking views during clear weather or behold fine, white mist swirling around the hills.

The Skywalk and Skydeck with elderly-friendly and disabled-friendly features offer splendid 180° views of George Town.

As dusk sets in, watch George Town light up, providing a magical backdrop against the evening sky.

At Penang Hill, it is always an unforgettable, refreshing and enriching experience.

Fauna/Flora

The most common forest type found on Penang Hill is the hill dipterocarp forest.


Penang Hill is scientifically important as a type site of many Malaysian plant species. In the past, botanists came here to collect plants for herbaria around the world. It is an area rich in biodiversity and has a great number of endemic species, some of which are so rare that their existence is endangered.

Growing at higher altitudes are some of the submontane oak-laurel as well as coniferous trees. The Tree fern which is normally associated with higher elevations is also found growing here.

Large mammals are not found on Penang Hill. Wild boars, small and medium-sized mammals such as the squirrels, monkeys and tree shrews can be seen.

Penang Hill has a rich bird fauna. Over 100 species or about 80% of the birds found on Penang Island have been recorded here. They range from the common garden species to rare deep forest inhabitants.

In the evenings, the characteristic calls of the cicadas and crickets are often heard. If one is discerning enough, the stick insect and leaf mantis may be found well-camouflaged among the vegetation.

Food

Penang Laksa

Penang Laksa (Laksa Pulau Pinang), also known as Asam Laksa, a specialty of the Malaysian island of Penang.

Fruit Rojak

This popular Malay snack is a must-eat Penang street food. Made using a collection of Malaysian fruits that are served in a sweet, sticky sauce and topped with roasted nuts, it is a form of Asian fruit salad.

Char Kuey Teow

Char kuey teow, Malaysia's most famous street food, but in Penang there's most popular is Ah-Nui Char kuey teow, as know as Char keuy teow with duck eggs, also in the Penang it's taste more spicy.
